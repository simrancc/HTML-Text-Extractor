Privacy Policy

  1. Introduction

    For purposes of this Privacy Policy, references to “we,” “our,” and “us” mean Ripl, Inc. Ripl, Inc. owns and operates the Ripl mobile app, the website at www.ripl.com, and the Ripl software service, which provides digital media creation, editing, publishing, and engagement tracking analytics services (the “Ripl Service”). This Privacy Policy explains how and why we collect, use, and disclose information about you collected through the Ripl Service.

    We reserve the right to change, modify, add, or remove portions of this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will not, however, retroactively change how we share your personal information without your consent. We will provide notice of any material changes through the Ripl Services or by other means as we deem appropriate before they take effect. Please check back periodically for any changes we may make to the Privacy Policy. Your continued use of the Ripl Service following the posting of changes to this Privacy Policy means you accept these changes.

  2. Personal Information We Collect

    When you interact with the Ripl Service, we may collect personal information, such as:

    • Name
    • Email address
    • Phone number
    • IP address
    • Mobile device identifier
    • Password
    • Geolocation data

    We may also collect general information about the equipment you use to access the Ripl Service, your operating system and browser type, the services that you use, times and dates of your interactions with the Ripl Service, details of your transactions through the Ripl Service, and mobile and web software event data.

  3. How We Collect Information

    We may collect information:

    • Directly from you when you provide it, including by filling out forms, setting up a personal profile, participating in sweepstakes, contests, or promotions, or contacting us through our “Contact Us” form.
    • Automatically as you interact with the Ripl Service, such as location data, data on browsing activities and patterns, times and dates of logins, and event data.
    • That you share, post, or transmit when interacting with the Ripl Service.
    • From third parties, including social media platforms and cloud storage services you use to interact with the Ripl Service.

    Social Media Platforms. The Ripl Service allows you to combine content you have stored on third-party cloud storage services and post it to social media platforms. When you post content to a social media platform using the Ripl Service, Ripl may have access to information from your social media account, such as likes and comments on posted content, and page or group affiliations. This information allows Ripl to provide you with personalized in-app recommendations. Most social media platforms provide you with a tool to control the information you share with the Ripl Service, which is governed by the policies of the applicable social media platform.

    Cookies. The Ripl Service uses cookies. A "cookie" is a small file that we save on your computer's hard drive, which contains the user’s IP address. Our cookies help to give you faster access to pages you have already visited, allow you to personalize your pages, and help you to participate in activities or events on the Ripl Service. They also help us customize our offerings to you. You may choose to disable cookies through your web browser, but doing so may affect your ability to access or use the Ripl Service.

    Web Beacons. Web beacons are small graphic images or other programming code (also known as "1×1 GIFS" or "clear GIFs"). We may include web beacons in the Ripl Service pages and email messages. Web beacons may be used to count visitors to the Ripl Service, to monitor how users navigate the Ripl Service, and to count how many emails that were sent were actually opened.

    We may use your information to:

    • Provide the service you've requested or agreed to receive.
    • Enable you to participate in Ripl Service features.
    • Enable you to participate in sweepstakes, contests, and promotions through the Ripl Service.
    • Tailor the content you see when you visit the Ripl Service.

    • Contact you with regard to your use of the Ripl Service.
    • Market Ripl products and services to you and send you marketing materials that may be of interest to you, but only if you consent to providing your information for this purpose.
    • Conduct promotional, analytical, and business development activities with Ripl’s trusted third-party partners.
    • For other purposes with your consent.

    Information You Share With Us. When you interact with the Ripl Service, you may set up your personal profile, add and post content, and transmit information through your social media profiles or email accounts. We may retain this information so that, for instance, you can return to view prior information you have sent and monitor and interact with content you have posted.

If you choose to opt out of Ripl’s marketing communications, you may still access the Ripl Service, but you may not receive important information about features and functionality of the Ripl Service or other products that may interest you.

The Ripl Service allows you to post content (User Submissions) publicly through third-party sites and services (including but not limited to social media sites). Subject to your profile and privacy settings for the applicable third-party site or service, any User Submissions that you make public are visible to and searchable by others. If you remove information that you posted, copies may remain viewable in cached and archived pages, or if others have copied or saved that information. Please see our Terms of Service for more information about User Submissions. In addition to User Submissions, the Ripl Service may allow you to post comments in public areas, such as social media networks, community pages, message boards, status updates, or feedback sections. Due to their public nature, User Submissions and comments you share in public areas are not subject to this Privacy Policy. Please be careful about what you choose to share publicly.

We reserve the right to disclose and transfer all information related to the Ripl Service, including, without limitation, your personal information, demographic information and usage information: (i) to a subsequent owner, co-owner or operator of the Ripl Service or applicable database; or (ii) in connection with a corporate merger, consolidation, restructuring, the sale of substantially all of our stock and/or assets or other corporate change, including, without limitation, during the course of any due diligence process.

We retain your personal information for as long as we deem appropriate under our internal data retention policy, or until you request that we delete it. In certain cases, Ripl may be required to retain your data to comply with applicable law or in connection with a legal claim.

    Lawful Bases. We collect and process your personal information only when we have a lawful basis to do so. Lawful bases include your consent, the fulfillment of Ripl’s contractual rights or obligations, complying with applicable law, and the legitimate interests of Ripl or third parties (provided that processing your data for a legitimate interest does not outweigh your rights and freedoms). Examples of a legitimate interest include (i) analyzing user data to maintain and improve the Ripl Service for all users; (ii) providing you with access to the Ripl Service; (iii) providing you with up-to- date information about new features of the Ripl Service; and (iv) engaging third-party providers to deliver certain features of the Ripl Service. You may withdraw your consent or object to a legitimate interest at any time by contacting us at the information provided below.

    Cross-Border Transfer. The Ripl Service is hosted on US servers. Any personal information you provide to us will be processed and stored on servers in the US, the laws of which may be deemed by other countries to have inadequate data protection. Accordingly, if you are located outside the US, you consent and continue to consent to the processing and storage of such data in the US. We have agreements in place with our affiliates and contractors that include standard contractual clauses to protect your rights with respect to your data.

    Do Not Track: Some browsers incorporate a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that, when turned on, signals to websites and online services that you do not want to be tracked. At this time, the Ripl Service does not respond to DNT signals.

    Children Under 16. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under the age of 16 and do not target the Ripl Service to children under 16. If you are under 16, please do not use the Ripl Service or submit any personal information through the Ripl Service. If you have reason to believe that a child under the age of 16 has provided their personal information to us, please contact us, and we will delete that information.
